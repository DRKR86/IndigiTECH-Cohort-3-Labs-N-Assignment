Five Places To Take A Mountain Bike adventure

Moab, Utah

Moab is a mountain biker's dream, set amid Utah's red rock desert with an otherworldly landscape of mesas, canyons, and ancient rock formations. Riders from around the world flock to Moab for the famous Slickrock Bike Trail, which feels almost like riding on the surface of Mars. The Whole Enchilada trail is a must for adventurous souls, combining high-alpine forests, red rock terrain, and desert valleys into one epic ride. After a day on the trails, bikers can unwind in Moab's welcoming town, full of local diners and outdoor adventure shops. Many travelers find that Moab’s unique scenery and challenging trails offer a transformative, almost surreal experience that keeps them coming back.

Whistler, Canada

For a biking trip filled with adrenaline and scenic beauty, Whistler in British Columbia has it all. The Whistler Bike Park is one of the best-maintained parks in the world, with everything from beginner-friendly green trails to heart-pounding black diamond runs. Many riders start with the famous A-Line, a flowy, jump-filled trail that’s practically a rite of passage. Outside of the park, alpine trails lead to quiet mountain lakes and panoramic views over the Coast Mountains. In the evenings, the village of Whistler buzzes with energy from a vibrant après-bike scene, where riders gather to share stories over local craft beers and meals.

French Alps, France

The French Alps offer an unforgettable mountain biking experience for those who seek high-altitude thrills and awe-inspiring natural beauty. With lift-accessed parks in Chamonix, Les Gets, and Morzine, bikers can enjoy long, exhilarating downhill rides with minimal climbs. Trails wind through fragrant pine forests, rocky terrain, and open meadows, providing breathtaking views of snow-capped peaks and deep valleys. Riders often enjoy combining a day on the trails with cultural exploration, stopping in nearby alpine villages for some French cuisine and local wine. This trip is ideal for bikers who want to mix their love of riding with cultural immersion and stunning scenery.

The Alps, New Zealand

New Zealand’s Southern Alps provide a thrilling, pristine setting for mountain bikers with a range of trails across varied landscapes. Trails here often begin near glaciers and weave down to lush valleys, offering everything from technical descents to scenic rides through alpine meadows. Mountain biking in the Alps offers a unique opportunity to experience New Zealand’s biodiversity, with trails that pass through subtropical forests, rocky highlands, and vast plains. Small towns at the base of these trails provide a friendly base for riders, and nearby hot springs make for the perfect way to relax after a day of biking.
